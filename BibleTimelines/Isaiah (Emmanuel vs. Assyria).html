He tells Ahaz that the plot of Aram and Israel against him will not succeed. Then he gives him the prophecy regarding Emmanuel, the son to be conceived by the virgin.

The sign of the prophecy of Emmanuel

  1. Concerns a time when "A remnant would have returned" symbolized in the name of Isaiah's son, Shear-Jashub, whom God instructs Isaiah to take along to meet King Ahaz [Isa 7:3]
  2. Is a sign to the house of David [Isa 7:13]

Hezekiah, Sennacherib and This Prophecy

We find a fulfilment of the Emmanuel prophecy in the days of Hezekiah, king of Judah

  1. Assyria’s Invasion of Judah: Years later, in the time of King Hezekiah of Judah, Sennacherib, King of Assyria, attacked Judah [Isa 36:1] coming as far as Jerusalem. This was just as prophesied that the Assyrian would come up to the neck of Judah [Isa 8:7-8].
  2. The Washerman’s Field: Sennacherib's field commandeer and army sent to Jerusalem stopped at the same place where God had told Isaiah, years before, to go to meet Ahaz, "...at the end of the aqueduct of the Upper Pool, on the road to the Washerman’s Field" [Isa 36:2; cf., Isa 7:3]
  3. The Remnant: At this time, the people of Judah who had not been captured by Sennacherib and those who had escaped to Jerusalem were the "Remnant" [Isa 37:4]. This "remnant" was saved just as prophesied in the name of Isaiah's son, "Shear-Jashub" which means, "A remnant will return". God had commanded Isaiah to go with his son, "Shear-Jashub", to meet King Ahaz.
  4. Emmanuel Saves Jerusalem: In the original prophecy, Emmanuel, the son born to the virgin, was their hope, he was to deliver Judah from the Assyrian invasion [Isa 8:8,9-10]. In this fulfilment, Judah was saved by the Angel of Yahweh [Isa 37:35-36]—“The Light of Israel… their Holy One…” [Isa 10:16-19]

The Tested & Precious Stone/Cornerstone

“See, I lay a Stone in Zion, a tested Stone, a Precious Cornerstone for a sure foundation; the one who trusts will never be dismayed” [Isa 28:16]

The Stone that Yahweh says He will lay in Zion is evidently a person. Just as with the more common usage of "pillar", a "Cornerstone" is can be, as in this case, representative of a leader in a community. For example, Isaiah speaks of the leaders of Egypt as her cornerstones [Isa 19:13].

This person, this Stone,

  1. Has been tested by Yahweh and
  2. Is precious to Yahweh.
  3. Calling him a cornerstone suggests that he was already acting as a leader of Israel

Since the One in focus as playing the role of a foundation, i.e., ruler for Israel is Emmanuel, the One to rule on David's Throne, this is Emmanuel.

The Stone Was Already in Existence!!

Saying that he has been tested implies that he was at the time this prophecy was spoken already in existence for only so could he have been 'tested'.

The plain reading of this prophecy implies that the beginning of Emmanuel's, this Stone's existence, is not in the future when he would be conceived by the virgin.

(But for the hindsight we now have through the New Testament, this plain meaning would have been hard, if not impossible, to even consider at the time.)

The Curse of Blindness

He said, “Go and tell this people:
“‘Be ever hearing, but never understanding; be ever seeing, but never perceiving.’ 10Make the heart of this people calloused; make their ears dull and close their eyes. Otherwise they might see with their eyes, hear with their ears, understand with their hearts, and turn and be healed.” [Isa 6:9-10]

Yahweh cursed the people with an inability to understand the message of the prophets.

  1. God curses them so that they would not  “...turn and be healed”
    1. That is God curses them so he would be able to carry out the sentence issued against them.
    2. The thing is that, if they turn to him in true repentance, God would have to forgive them and then he would not be able to carry out the sentence against them for then there would be no sin to punish.
    3. God wants his wrath to be known [Rom 9:22]
  2. This curse is not to last forever but until the day of Yahweh [Isa 6:11-13]
  3. This curse works through the people's failure to pay attention to the truth of God's words to them [Isa 42:18-20]
